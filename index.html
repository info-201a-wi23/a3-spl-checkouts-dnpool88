Introduction

The dataset I explored for this assignment was from the Seattle Public Library. I chose to analyze trends on checkouts at different time intervals because I wanted to see if seasons affected checkouts (using months) and how Covid affected the libraries (using years). I also wanted to look at Digital vs.Physical items and see if there was a difference there. Lastly, I chose a popular book (Educated : a memoir / Tara Westover.) and create a histogram to see how often the book was checked out by the month.

The Dataset

This data was collected by Seattle Public Libraries, whose dataset contains information dating back to 2005. The dataset I chose contains data on the year (2013-2023), month, # of checkouts for each item, whether the item was digital or physical, the author, the subject, the checkout type, the publisher and publication year, the ISBN, and the type of item (book, cd, etc.). According to their website, the data was collected to improve the quality of life for our residents; increasing transparency, accountability and comparability; promoting economic development and research; and improving internal performance management. The only ethical question I can think to consider is that the authors that are not listed in the database wont get the recognition they deserve, especially the authors of videos. A couple limitations/ problems with the dataset is that not all the data points are filled; there are a couple rows without an ISBN or an author, especially on videos. Another limitation is that there is no data on the Seattle Public Library locations, which I would find very interesting.
